# Industry Playbook

Pre-analyzed data for 10 high-opportunity industries. Use this to accelerate ideation and ground opportunities in real market intelligence. Each entry includes top manual workflows, known pain points, competition density, and buyer persona sketches.

## 1. Logistics & Supply Chain

**Top Manual Workflows**: Freight quote comparison, shipment tracking across carriers, customs documentation, proof-of-delivery reconciliation, warehouse inventory audits, route optimization spreadsheets.

**Pain Points**: Fragmented systems (TMS, WMS, ERP don't talk to each other), manual data entry between systems, delayed shipment visibility, customs compliance errors cost $5k-50k per incident, driver shortage makes dispatch optimization critical.

**Competition Density**: Moderate. Large players (FourKites, project44) serve enterprise. SMB logistics is underserved. Niche verticals (cold chain, last-mile, cross-border) have gaps.

**Buyer Personas**: Operations Manager at 3PL companies (50-500 employees), Logistics Coordinator at mid-market manufacturers, Freight Broker owner-operators.

---

## 2. Legal / Compliance

**Top Manual Workflows**: Contract review and redlining, due diligence document review, regulatory compliance tracking, legal research, client intake and conflict checks, billing time entry.

**Pain Points**: Junior associates spend 60%+ time on document review. Contract review backlogs delay deals. Compliance tracking across jurisdictions is error-prone. Missed deadlines = malpractice risk. Billing at $200-600/hr makes any time saved extremely valuable.

**Competition Density**: Growing. Players like Kira Systems, LawGeex, Harvey exist but mostly target large firms. Solo practitioners and mid-size firms are underserved. Niche practice areas (immigration, IP, real estate closings) have vertical opportunities.

**Buyer Personas**: Managing Partner at mid-size law firms (10-50 attorneys), General Counsel at mid-market companies, Compliance Officer at regulated industries.

---

## 3. Healthcare Administration

**Top Manual Workflows**: Patient intake forms, insurance eligibility verification, prior authorization, medical coding (ICD/CPT), claims submission, denial management, appointment scheduling, referral coordination.

**Pain Points**: Prior auth takes 15+ hours per physician per week. Claims denial rate is 5-10%, each re-submission costs $25-50 in admin time. Staff burnout from repetitive admin. Revenue cycle leakage from coding errors.

**Competition Density**: Moderate to crowded in RCM. Niche areas (prior auth, specific specialties, rural clinics) remain underserved. Regulatory complexity creates barriers that also protect moats.

**Buyer Personas**: Practice Manager at physician groups (5-30 providers), Revenue Cycle Director at community hospitals, Office Manager at specialty clinics.

---

## 4. Real Estate

**Top Manual Workflows**: Property listing descriptions, comparative market analysis (CMA), lease abstraction, tenant screening document review, property inspection reports, rental payment reconciliation, maintenance request triage.

**Pain Points**: Agents spend 5-10 hours/week on listing copy. CMAs require pulling and comparing 10-20 data points manually. Commercial lease abstraction takes 2-4 hours per lease. Maintenance coordination involves phone tag and spreadsheets.

**Competition Density**: Low to moderate for AI-specific tools. CRM space is crowded (Follow Up Boss, kvCORE) but AI-native workflow tools are sparse. Commercial real estate has more gaps than residential.

**Buyer Personas**: Broker/Owner at mid-size brokerages (20-100 agents), Property Manager managing 100-500 units, Commercial real estate analyst.

---

## 5. Construction

**Top Manual Workflows**: Project estimation and takeoffs, RFI (Request for Information) management, daily report compilation, safety compliance tracking, change order processing, subcontractor qualification, permit application preparation.

**Pain Points**: Estimation errors cost 5-15% of project value. RFIs create communication bottlenecks (average project has 200-800 RFIs). Daily reports are handwritten or dictated. Safety violations cost $15k-150k per OSHA citation. Change orders are processed manually and often disputed.

**Competition Density**: Low for AI tools. Legacy software (Procore, PlanGrid) dominates but is not AI-native. Massive opportunity for AI layers on top of existing tools. Subcontractor management is especially underserved.

**Buyer Personas**: Project Manager at GCs (general contractors), Estimator at specialty subcontractors, Safety Director at mid-size construction firms.

---

## 6. Accounting & Finance

**Top Manual Workflows**: Bank reconciliation, expense categorization, invoice processing, financial report preparation, tax document collection, audit preparation, accounts payable/receivable management.

**Pain Points**: Month-end close takes 5-10 days at most mid-market companies. Manual reconciliation is error-prone and boring. Tax season creates massive workload spikes. Audit prep requires assembling hundreds of documents.

**Competition Density**: Moderate. Big players (Xero, QuickBooks, Bill.com) exist but are not AI-native for complex tasks. Niche opportunities in specific verticals (restaurant accounting, e-commerce accounting, nonprofit compliance) and specific workflows (audit prep, tax document assembly).

**Buyer Personas**: Controller at mid-market companies (100-1000 employees), Partner at CPA firms (5-30 CPAs), Bookkeeper/accountant serving SMBs.

---

## 7. HR & Recruiting

**Top Manual Workflows**: Resume screening, interview scheduling, candidate sourcing, job description writing, onboarding checklist management, performance review compilation, benefits enrollment processing.

**Pain Points**: Recruiters spend 23 hours/week sourcing and screening. 75% of resumes are unqualified. Interview scheduling involves 5-10 emails per candidate. Performance reviews take managers 3-5 hours each to write. Compliance documentation (I-9, EEO) is tedious but critical.

**Competition Density**: Crowded in ATS and sourcing. Gaps remain in: screening for niche roles (trades, healthcare), onboarding automation for SMBs, performance review intelligence, and compliance-specific HR tasks.

**Buyer Personas**: HR Manager at companies (50-500 employees), Recruiting Agency owner (5-20 recruiters), Office Manager wearing HR hat at small businesses.

---

## 8. Insurance

**Top Manual Workflows**: Claims intake and FNOL (First Notice of Loss), policy comparison and quoting, underwriting data collection, claims adjuster reports, fraud detection review, renewal processing, compliance filing.

**Pain Points**: Claims processing takes 10-30 days on average. Manual data entry from FNOL to claims system. Underwriting requires pulling data from 5-10 sources. Fraud review is manual and catches only obvious cases. Policy renewals are processed reactively not proactively.

**Competition Density**: Moderate. InsurTech startups exist but mostly focus on distribution (Lemonade, Root). Back-office claims processing and underwriting automation remain underserved, especially for independent agencies and MGAs.

**Buyer Personas**: Claims Manager at regional carriers, Principal at independent insurance agencies (5-20 agents), Underwriting Manager at MGAs.

---

## 9. Agriculture

**Top Manual Workflows**: Crop scouting reports, pest/disease identification, yield estimation, compliance documentation (organic, GAP), supply chain paperwork, equipment maintenance scheduling, labor time tracking.

**Pain Points**: Crop scouting is time-intensive and subjective. Pest identification requires expertise not available on every farm. Compliance documentation for organic certification takes 20+ hours per season. Equipment downtime from reactive maintenance costs $1k-10k/day. Labor management across seasonal workers is chaotic.

**Competition Density**: Low. AgTech is mostly hardware/IoT (sensors, drones). AI-native software for farm management workflows is sparse. Niche opportunities in specialty crops, organic compliance, and food safety documentation.

**Buyer Personas**: Farm Manager at mid-size operations (500-5000 acres), Agricultural Consultant serving multiple farms, Compliance Manager at food processing companies.

---

## 10. Education & Training

**Top Manual Workflows**: Lesson plan creation, assignment grading and feedback, student progress report writing, IEP (Individualized Education Program) documentation, accreditation compliance, curriculum alignment mapping, parent communication.

**Pain Points**: Teachers spend 7-10 hours/week on grading and feedback. IEP writing takes 3-5 hours per student. Report card comments are repetitive but expected to be personalized. Curriculum alignment across standards is manual spreadsheet work. Accreditation prep takes months of document assembly.

**Competition Density**: Low for AI tools beyond basic grading. Heavy regulation and procurement complexity in K-12. Higher ed and corporate training are faster to sell into. Niche opportunities in IEP writing, accreditation prep, and assessment creation.

**Buyer Personas**: Department Head at K-12 schools, Instructional Designer at corporate training teams, Administrator at private/charter schools (faster procurement).

## How to Use This Playbook

1. When a user specifies an industry, start with the relevant entry above
2. Cross-reference the manual workflows with the user's specific interest
3. Use the competition density to calibrate the "differentiation potential" score
4. Use the buyer persona to ground the opportunity in a real decision-maker
5. If the industry isn't listed here, apply the same analytical framework: identify workflows, pain points, competition, and buyers through reasoning
